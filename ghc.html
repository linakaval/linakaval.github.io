Lina's Tips for GHC

If you're a woman/nonbinary in tech reading this, hopefully you already know about GHC (Grace Hopper Celebration). It's an amazing conference for networking and career development (not to mention all the free swag if you're lucky enough to attend in-person!).

Keep reading to learn all my tips and tricks for making the most of GHC!

How to prepare (In-Person & Remote)

  1. UPLOAD YOUR RESUME TO THE ANITA B DATABASE

    2. THIS IS SO IMPORTANT THAT IT IS IN ALL UPPERCASE LOL. Many companies will p

  2. Update your resume!

    3. Make sure you put your most recent projects, internships, awards, etc.

  3. Update your website!

    4. I put my website link on my resume, and it is nice to have things to show if interviewers or recruiters ask about projects!

  4. Update your LinkedIn!

    5. Many people who attend GHC will add "#GHC23" or whatever year to their LinkedIn headline. It makes it easy to connect with other attendees and companies will also be active on LinkedIn (so connect with them there too!).

  5. Practice your pitch

    6. I know this is cliche, but you need to be able to pitch yourself confidently and effectively. Try to find one thing about the recruiter that you can relate to or talk about something other than internships/careers! I found that my most meaningful connections came from meeting people while in line to take pics in front of the GHC sign or by talking about a personal aspect of my life.

How to make the most of GHC

  • Hit the career fair

    • If you are a senior about to graduate, it is in your best interest to hit all the booths you are interested in and network. Because I was able to secure my full-time offer at GHC my senior year, I was able to go the rest of my year not having to worry about graduating with no job. I got to enjoy my senior year focusing on both classes and my friends.

  • Don't be afraid to make friends

    • Connections can come from everywhere. The best thing about GHC is that there are so many knowledgable, succesful, and aspirational women at this conference.
